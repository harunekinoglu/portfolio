Beyazit Square was, and is, one of the most historical and symbolic squares of Istanbul Peninsula, throughout history. The square exists on the third hill of the Historic Peninsula (Freely, 2011, p. 183). The historical significance of the square dates back to the Roman period and known as Forum Tauri and Forum Theodosius in Byzantium period (Ayvazoglu, 2012) (Müller-Wiener, 2002). The square exists at the intersection of significant urban elements that make historic Istanbul image.

Beyazit Square experienced acute demolishing interventions from the mid-20th century onwards urbanization resulting in vast destruction of the existing built city form. Scholars (Müller-Wiener, 2002), (Eyice & Kuban, 1993), (Ayvazoglu, 2012) note that the most destructive intervention upon the square occurred in the 1950s through the implementation of the Prost plan, which gave priority to the motor-vehicle traffic. The enlargement and downing of the Ordu Street in the southbound of the square resulted in not only a massive fragmentation between the street and the square but also a remarkable change in the adjacent built environment of the square. Besides, the northwest access to the square, Vezneciler Street was also enlarged through a vast amount of massive destruction as shown in Figure. Today, the architectural elements that make the square’s identity are foremost the Istanbul University’s main gate, the Beyazid Mosque Complex, the State Library of Beyazid and the Madrasa (Ayvazoglu, 2012). Spatial assessment reports prepared by several public authorities point out to the poor spatial organization of the square due to the remarkable change that the square faced within the last decades. Briefly, Beyazıt Square is the most prominent public space of the historic center of İstanbul. Over the last century, the square has lost its spatial wholeness with the environs, and become functionally obscure, causing the square to be isolated and neglected space. Although its highly central location, today the square is far from functioning as a vibrant public space.

Urban Design Project

The design approach was developed to rearticulate and promote the potential of the entire area to establish a smooth relationship with the urban character and the fragmented structure in the vicinity.

The project in collaboration with various stakeholder aimed to develop a holistic design approach for the square that undertakes the square’s great historical background, present dynamics, obstacles, potentials and future envisioning in Istanbul Peninsula and entire metropolitan area. The landmark square lays in the intersection of masterpiece entrance of Istanbul University at the north, Ordu the Ottoman imperial street at the south, The Grand Bazaar, one of the oldest and largest grand bazaars of the world at the west, and Suleimaniye neighborhood where the Great Architect Sinan’s masterpiece Suleimanie mosque exists at the east.

The project aims at resolving the spatial and functional problems of the square, by a holistic approach, and a design project that aims at a modern and accessible public space that highlights the historical significance of the Beyazıt Complex and Beyazıt Square.

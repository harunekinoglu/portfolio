Odakule passage belongs to the Istanbul Chamber of Industry located in the heart of Beyoglu district, aka Pera, in the old Istanbul. The building is located on one of the busiest pedestrian streets of Istanbul; Istıklal Street. The building, in the 1970s, is the very first example of high rise modern architecture in Istanbul. Greeting post-modernism, the high rise building exhibits a contextual attitude, not dominating its plot but taking a chance to create an inner public space in the street level connecting the whole-day dense pedestrian flow of Istiklal street to the Mesrutiyet street.


Another remarkable feature to mention is; the main trunk of the building in the upper levels centralizes itself in the very center leaving blank space in the front and back facades. Such manner makes a modest yet elegant embodiment responding to the question of how to high-rise in the historic Beyoglu urban texture. The purpose of the competition is to seek contemporary and innovative design proposals for the passage connecting two dense streets with a prior aim to promote the spatial quality and publicness and re-integrate it to the urban living around.

Design proposal: Interactive Museum of Crafts & Design

Istanbul, entitled as UNESCO City of Design since 2017, hosts a deep and rich creative background mostly remaining from Classical Ottoman craftsmanships, creative practices, manufacturings. The Grand Bazaar in the old Istanbul, a.k.a. Historical Peninsula, hosted more than 550 different types of craftsmanship in its golden era from 16th to the 20th century. Very few of those craftsmanships survived in the old Istanbul, especially in the old neighborhoods of the Historical Peninsula and Beyoglu districts, and made to this day from several hundred years before. These networks are trying hard to cope with modern day design practices, mass manufacturing, and supply & demand circles, which heavily dominated by the products imported from China. Unless some promoting and protective policies are urgently applied, the knowledge and the practices of several craftsmanships are fading away towards an inevitable end.


The proposal of this project is built upon an innovative spatial and creative program to heal the loss that this dilemma creates. Although the design brief of the competition does not expect so, the design proposal has been developed to re-think and re-function the passage not only on the street level but also on the mezzanine level which are two vacant spaces currently. On the ground level, the passage is considered to function as an exhibition corridor decorated with design elements on the walls along the corridor. The ground level, hanged upon the façades of both mezzanines and cylindrical columns by both entrances, are installed led-screens to screen the agenda of the interactive design and craftsmanship museum proposed for the mezzanine.


The interactive design and craftsmanship museum proposed for the mezzanine is cyberspace. The museum heavily uses the means of digital technology to allow visitors to experience design through the various practices of craftsmanship virtually through VR (Virtual Reality) and AR (Augmented Reality) technology.

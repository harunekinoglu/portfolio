In 2009 Kayabasi, a new housing area that exists in the western fringe of Istanbul, through architecture and landscape design project competition was aimed to be a sustainable residential development area that exists in the west of the European part of Istanbul. Istanbul, especially in the last two decades, has been experiencing a rapid sprawl characterized by new housing zones both in the east and west axis. Kayabasi is one of the west-end housing zones built within the last two decades led by the Mass Housing Administration (TOKI). The Kayabasi area is in the periphery yet adjacent to densely populated residential and commercial areas with easy access to multi-modal transportation infrastructures. These features of the area would bring threats towards a rapid, unplanned & unhealthy urbanization unless a comprehensive and sustainable master plan with needed architectural and landscape proposals developed and implemented by the local government and investors. The master plan with architectural and landscape design proposals developed for the area by our design team developed, Ekinoglu & Sezer, a project namely “green mile.” The proposal defines a strong ideology of environmental and social sustainability re-constructed through empowering more use of outdoors for a healthy outdoor culture through architecture and landscape to benefit environmental sources such as solar, rain, the wind that will refresh our daily indoor & outdoors interactions and provide mental, physical and societal well-being in return.

Yenikapı Transfer Point and Archaeo-Park Area | Urban Design

Yenikapi in Historical Peninsula and its influence area

Background

The Yenikapi project originates from an exceptional archaeological finding. In 2004, during the construction of an undersea tunnel planned to connect the Asian and the European sides of Istanbul, an outstanding archaeological site emerged from the mud. Remains dating back to the Neolithic age, a Byzantine harbour, Ottoman structures and 35 shipwrecks were discovered. An International architecture competition was organized in 2012 so to identify a proper design for the entire site.

International Urban Design Competition addressing Istanbul’s Yenikapi District in the Historic Peninsula of Istanbul has been organized as a joint operation by Istanbul Metropolitan Municipality and Istanbul 2010 European Capital of Culture Agency. Except for the design process, one of my key and critical role was to manage the periodic workshops with all the concerning bodies and stakeholders for liaising their contributions in order to develop a wholly integrated urban design brief for the Waterfront-Multi Faceted Transfer Point where several subway lines intersect, while ensuring careful archeological preservation of its surrounding “Archeo-Park Area,” which research indicates may date back to 8,500 years. Excavations undertaken during the application of the transportation projects, both the remains of a 1600-year-old “Theodosius Port” and also with the “35 antique shipwreck remains” of the World’s largest fleet collection were unearthed. The competition process had two phases. First phase was the assessment of all the submissions including their visionary approaches by the technical committee, where one of the member was myself, in order to present them to the selection committee, consisting of the worldwide prominent architects, urban designers and thinkers including Massimiliano FUKSAS, Michael SORKIN, Odile DECQ, Haluk PAMIR, Enzo SIVIERO and Süha OZKAN. Second phase was the final stage of presentations of the 9 finalist projects to the Selection Committee and Mayor in a public meeting in Istanbul.

Protecting the Archaeological Area while continue the transportation system development become a challenge of this project.

Design Process

The following items determind whether the process is open to the participation of different factors:

  • The potential of the entire area to establish smooth relationship witht eh urban character and the fragmented structure in the vincinity
  • The programs with which it is equpped with as a public space, its flexiblity to allow momentary/spontaneous activities; thus its potential to host diverse and unplanned events
  • Organization of open areas and their relationship with the coast and the dense urban fabric in the background
  • Whether there is a memorable image of the entire area and the relationship of that image with the Historical Peninsula as a whole

Design Solution

In this sense, design will have an interlocking three-layered structure:

  • An "Urban Vision" Study which positions Yenikapi in both the Historical Peninsula and Istanbul
  • An "Urban Design" Study explaining the organization of the entire area as a public space and the relationships with its immediate srroundings
  • An "Architectural Project" illustrating the richness of the program, organizations of locations and the architectural characteristics in the relocation to the Transfer Center and City Archive buildings

Design Proposal hightlights:


  • Considering the current dynamic of the city, positioning the area from Aksaray Square to the Coast of Marmara and from Mustafa Kemal Boulevard to the Cerrahpasa Campus of Istanbul University as the new" city center" within the Istanbul macro form and Historical Peninsula
  • Shaping the new city center with the specific characteristics of the area, whilst at the same time establishing a model for the potential city centers to be formed in the intersection of the new backbone of the city determined by the Marmaray's route on the east-west axis and the transport lines in the north-south direction.
  • It is required that the new center will act as and facilitate solution ot the social and spatial polarization, since it is a major problem of the global cities, is built based on spatial and programmatic organization, the center is positioned as a public space in both Historical Peninsula and the Istanbul macro form and the relationship between the transformation airea and the silhouette of the Historical Peninsula is problematized
  • Considering a model to solve the common problem of the entire Historial Peninsula: dense highway traffic which prevents pedestrians from accessing to sea transport: it is excepted that a pedestrian connection from Aksaray Square to the Marmara Sea is established and certain mixed-use program approaches are develop to reinforce this connection and to maintain the dynamics of the area over 24 hours
  • It is required that the optimum denseness which will ensure the dynamics and the economical sustainability of the area is realized, the surrounding programs and pedestrian flow are carried inside and maintained there

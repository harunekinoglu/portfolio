The Competition:

The urban design competition was held by the Mersin Metropolitan Municipality open to the professionals of the disciplines from Architecture, Landscape Architecture, and City and Regional Planning as national and one-stage idea competition in mid-2018.

The area:

The 77.7-hectare urban area includes sub-areas with different functions. İsmet İnönü Boulevard, one of the leading transport backbone of the city, divides the competition area into two;
1-Tevfik Sırrı Gür Stadium, which is not anymore actively used due to the opening of the new stadium, 2-The section where the Muftu River begins to end, the military areas (military branch and lodgings), the natural and historical sites, and the remaining part of Atatürk Street in the Çamlıbel district, and the Republic Square.
Additionally, in the southern part of the competition area, in the coastal band; there are military areas, lighthouse, Mersin Water and Sewerage Administration (MESKI) treatment plant, a fishing shelter which is thought to serve elsewhere, and an amusement park that is expected to be re-integrated with the Recreation Area.
The urban area framed for an urban design competition has a variety of functions with major chronic issues that are making of a fragmented urban environment and lowering the accessibility, safety, and healthy urban living standards. The urban design competition seeks to find design solutions to these issues and re-function the ex-stadium through a holistic proposal that re-connects the entire waterfront back to urban life.

Design Proposal:

We as a team of six, consisting of architects, urban planners, and landscape architect, developed our urban design proposal with the following principles:

  • Establishing north & south and east & west connections
  • making of limiting and disconnecting edges into transparent spaces and functional nodes
  • re-establishing of the long lost urban and seaside interactions
  • setting urban sustainability strategies for energy-efficiency in various scales to monitor regularly in order to measure and update
  • creating new-urban interfaces that are inclusive enough to promote urbanity and urban-identity and sense of belonging
